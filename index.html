The Jacket That Had To Be Praised

Complimenting that jacket was the excuse. You were always the reason. That one conversation was all it took for a day to become a memory I'd cherish forever. All I could do back then was admire the subtle strength in your presence, quietly and completely. Some people enter a room. You entered my story.

The Queen’s Coronation

The way you recited that poem was pure magic. The crowd went silent, my heart froze, and I knew I had just witnessed something unforgettable. That day, you made two hearts fall for you—mine, and Kajal Miss’s. I can never forget the time spent in afterparty. The tarot reader and my privilege to lift your belongings.

Upscaling The Game

I somehow convinced the most intimidating woman I'd ever met to go on a date with me… by trading it for THE 4K video. I asked you out without knowing where to take you, and you ended up dropping me to the metro, and oh how my wallet gave me a proper scare. Everything went hilariously wrong, yet somehow, it all felt perfect. And those chunky Converse? I still don’t know if I like them hehe.

4 July 2024

The Best Day On Earth

I still giggle thinking about that day. Me wearing my brand new white shoes in heavy rain just to impress you, while your friends practically threatened my life before we even met. And then I saw you in that beautiful green dress, and my heart totally forgot how to beat. Sitting beside you, talking for hours, feeling the warmth of your presence, I knew I had chosen the best day to ask you out. And the way you laughed at my terrible attempt, only to tell me to do it again properly. Those hours with you told me everything I needed to know, and left me certain of exactly what I wanted.

CHAPTER FIVE // FROM ME TO YOU

A Note for the Record

Click the wax seal below to open your letter.

Dear Saps,

Two years ago today, I did something I'd been too nervous to do for months, and I’m always going to hold you for making me to it again. I've been thinking about that a lot lately. How the day everyone else remembers as your birthday is also the day I remember as the start of the best decision I've ever made.

Our journey didn’t begin by the textbook definition, if it did, it wouldn’t have been so magical. You were up on a stage singing the most beautiful poem ever made and being announced the Student of the Year, and I was at the end of the other side, behind the crowd, absorbing your radiance, thinking if there is any way I can come and help you with mic again as you were reciting the poem, just so I can witness your performance closely.

There's a memory I keep returning to, from the night we got caught in the rain on the way back from our pottery date. You climbed into the cab, tucked yourself under my arm without asking, and stayed there the whole ride. You were tired and snuggled like a baby. I remember it as one of the clearest moments I've had, this quiet understanding that I wanted to be the person you reach for when things get complicated, even something as small as the weather.

You rub your nose a little extra when you're studying, or get irritated at something, or right before you say something smarter than whatever I was about to say. Your real laugh, not the polite one you use with people you've just met, is loud enough that our entire friend group can pick you out of another room without seeing you. You hit me, gently, every time I tease you, which is often, but I keep doing it because your reaction is one of my favourite parts of any given day. You light up over food the way some people light up over good news, and you will not be interrupted mid-romcom for any reason short of an actual emergency. None of this is a secret to the people who know you. I think I'm just the one who's spent two years quietly keeping track of all of it.

You are, without exaggeration, the most mature person in our age when it comes to knowing how the world works. You inspire me with how you actually carry things, or how you fight some silent wars. Calmly, thoroughly, and without needing anyone to notice how hard you're working. I've watched you build a work ethic most people only ever talk about. I notice it. I don't think I say that enough.

Every time life asked more of you, you quietly became more capable of meeting it. You don't always notice yourself changing. I do. And if these two years are any indication, you're nowhere near done growing, which mostly just means I get to keep watching, which might be my favourite part of all of this.

What you need to know is that whatever this year looks like, and the one after it, and the ones after that, I plan on being there for all of it. Not telling you this because it's your birthday and I'm supposed to say that, but because two years in, it is still the easiest decision I make every single day.

So happy birthday, Saps. Happy two years since I got lucky enough to hear you say yes (the second time). I hope today gives you every small thing you love… good food, a movie you've maybe seen twice, a laugh loud enough that passersby wonder who it belongs to. And somewhere in the middle of all of it, I hope you feel, even for a second, exactly how I've felt for two years straight, like the luckiest version of myself.

Yours,
JSR
